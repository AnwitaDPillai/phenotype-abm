1. Abstract

Phenotype is a state-of-the-art spatial, non-linear Agent-Based Model (ABM) analyzing the emergence of cooperative structures under continuous environmental, genetic, and institutional feedback loops. By abandoning rigid, discrete strategy classifications, this platform simulates strategy evolution along a continuous epigenetic spectrum mapped across a spatial thermodynamic matrix.

2. Epigenetic Genomic Strand

Each autonomous entity possesses an embedded chromosome vector determining behavioral phenotypes based on metabolic stress markers:

$$\mathbf{G} = [G_1(\text{Altruism}), G_2(\text{Forgiveness}), G_3(\text{Desperation})]$$

When an agent's internal reserve drops below the threshold dictated by $G_3$, its social framework undergoes a non-linear state mutation, switching dynamically to parasitic extraction models to secure biological survival.

4. Graph Topology Defenses

Mutual cooperative links establish dynamic spatial graph edges inside a real-time adjacency matrix. High-density kinship clusters propagate threat vectors across the network topology, triggering defensive pre-emptive lockouts against incoming predatory defector vectors.
